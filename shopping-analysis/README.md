# Shopping Analysis

## Project Overview

This project performs **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a shopping dataset to understand product performance, customer behavior, pricing patterns, and category-level trends. The analysis includes data cleaning, feature engineering, visualization, and business insights derived from the dataset.

### Key Objectives

* Analyze product ratings and customer engagement.
* Study the impact of pricing and discounts on product performance.
* Identify popular and high-performing product categories.
* Create meaningful visualizations to uncover trends and patterns.
* Generate business insights to support data-driven decision making.

---

## Dataset Summary

* **Total Records:** 1,000 Products
* **Total Features:** 24 Columns
* **Data Types:** Numerical and Categorical
* **Key Variables:** Rating, Ratings Count, Initial Price, Final Price, Discount, Category

---

## Key Findings

* Most products have high ratings, indicating overall customer satisfaction.
* Ratings count is a stronger indicator of product popularity than ratings alone.
* There is no strong relationship between product price and customer ratings.
* Discounts influence product prices but have little effect on customer ratings.
* Customer demand is concentrated in a few high-performing categories.

---

## Business Implications

* Evaluate products using both ratings and ratings count.
* Adopt flexible pricing strategies without worrying about rating impact.
* Use discounts primarily to increase sales and attract customers.
* Focus marketing and inventory efforts on top-performing categories.
* Encourage customer reviews to improve product credibility and visibility.

---

## Conclusion

The analysis shows that product popularity is driven more by customer engagement and review volume than by price or discount levels. While most products receive positive ratings, businesses should focus on increasing customer trust, improving review participation, and investing in high-performing categories. Combining rating metrics, pricing strategies, and category analysis can help organizations make more informed business decisions and achieve sustainable growth.
